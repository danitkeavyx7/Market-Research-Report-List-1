<p><p>The regenerative therapies market includes allografts, xenografts, and alloplasts, each serving unique purposes in tissue repair and replacement. Allografts involve transplanting tissues from a donor of the same species, offering compatibility with minimal immune response. Xenografts use tissues from different species, often requiring modifications to enhance acceptance in human patients. Alloplasts are synthetic materials designed to replace biological tissues, providing flexibility in applications where natural grafts are unsuitable. Each type plays a crucial role in advancing regenerative medicine and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
