<p><p>The Chip Thick Film Resistor Market includes two primary types: Through Hole and Surface Mount Device (SMD) resistors. Through Hole resistors are mounted on a printed circuit board via drilled holes, offering robust mechanical strength, making them suitable for high-power applications. In contrast, SMD resistors are designed for direct mounting on the board's surface, enabling compact designs and automated assembly processes. The growing demand for miniaturization and efficiency in electronics drives the preference for SMD resistors in modern applications.</p></p>
