<p><p>Pulsed radar systems operate across various frequency bands, each serving distinct applications. The L Band (1–2 GHz) is ideal for long-range detection and weather monitoring. The S Band (2–4 GHz) is commonly used for air traffic control and maritime surveillance due to its balance of range and resolution. The C Band (4–8 GHz) is effective for weather radar and satellite communications, while the X Band (8–12 GHz) excels in high-resolution imaging and target tracking in defense and security applications.</p></p>
